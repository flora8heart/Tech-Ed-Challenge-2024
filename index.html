♥ About Me ♥

Hi! I'm Michelle.

I enjoy problem-solving and solving puzzles, which is one of the reasons I am drawn towards programming and computer science. On the creative side, I have a strong passion and love for music. I have been playing piano since I was five and I love to sing. I can play a little bit of guitar too, but piano has always been my main passion.

The reason I am doing this software development course is because I am pursuing a career switch after a decade in the pharmacy and healthcare sector. This includes nearly 6 years of working as a pharmacist, 4 years of pharmacy master's degree and 1 year of pre-registration training.

When I was 17 years old, I was at a crossroad trying to decide which degree to study upon the completion of my A-Levels studies. My interest at the time was pursuing a career that will utilise either a background in science or mathematics. I was on the fence between computer science, aerospace engineering or pharmacy. At the time in 2012, the IT world was not the way it is now and one of the fears my parents had was that pursuing computer science would mean that I may not have a stable career prospects. Being so young and naive, societal and family pressure meant that I was led down the "safe" path, and thus I decided to pursue pharmacy.

When I was in university studying for my pharmacy master's degree, I always felt like something was missing and will seek opportunities to somehow immerse myself in mathematics or tech. For example, when I was learning about a rate-limiting step formula of a chemical reaction during a physical pharmaceutics lecture, I remember spending time in class figuring out how the mathematical formula was derived. I did not stop until I was able to figure out how it was derived even though it is not part of the learning material and thoroughly enjoyed the process.

During my final year project, I remembered vividly that I was getting frustrated with keeping track of multiple versions of my file documents. I was making multiple copies and naming these files as "final_project_v2.docx", "final_project_FINAL_FINAL.docx" etc. Eventually, I realised that this was getting out of hand and thought that it was an inefficient method to keep track of which was the most recent version of my file.

Thus, I set out to figure out a way to solve this problem and this was how I came across Git and thought this will be a great tool to use for version control for my final year project. From here, I learned about Markdown and saved my documents in Markdown format. I also came across Bibtex for academic referencing and learned how to use Pandoc for final output of my thesis in PDF or DOCX format for final submission. I was very proud of myself for figuring this out, and was able to find a little joy in my degree despite it not being my first choice.

After graduating from university, I continued down the path of practicing as a pharmacist. Now in 2024, I feel complete with what I have achieved as a pharmacist and I decided that it is time to pursue the other passion I had when I was seventeen, which was programming, thus why I was drawn towards this course. I look forward to meeting other liked-minded people at the bootcamp, and looking forward to learning and working with others in a collaborative environment.
